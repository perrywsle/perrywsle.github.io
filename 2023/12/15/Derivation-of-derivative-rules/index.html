Power rule

Simple expansion of (x + change of x)^n

(x+dx)n=(x+dx)(x+dx)(x+dx)...(x+dx)=xn+nxn−1dx(x+dx)^n=(x+dx)(x+dx)(x+dx)...(x+dx)\newline=x^n+nx^{n-1}dx (x+dx)n=(x+dx)(x+dx)(x+dx)...(x+dx)=xn+nxn−1dx

there will be multiples of dx^2 from the expansion but is negligible cause the value of dx^2 is too small
x^n :derived out simply
x^(n-1) :derived as following:

xn−1dx=(dx∗x...x)+(x∗dx...x)+...(x∗x...dx)x^{n-1}dx=(dx*x...x)+(x*dx...x)+...(x*x...dx) xn−1dx=(dx∗x...x)+(x∗dx...x)+...(x∗x...dx)

there is n times of (dx * x …* x), thus:

nxn−1nx^{n-1} nxn−1
